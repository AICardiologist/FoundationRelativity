Title: Fermat Domination and the Variational Hodge Conjecture: A CRMLint Squeeze (Paper 88, Constructive Reverse Mathematics Series)

Author: Paul Chun-Kit Lee

Affiliation: New York University, Brooklyn, New York

Date: February 2026

Description:
Paper 88 of the Constructive Reverse Mathematics (CRM) Series. We address the asymmetric genus-4 hyperelliptic family C_t: y^2 = x^9 + tx^7 + x from Paper 85, whose Jacobian is generically absolutely simple. Unlike the symmetric family of Paper 86, no reciprocal involution exists (the core polynomial x^8 + tx^6 + 1 is not palindromic), so the Kani-Rosen splitting is unavailable.

We prove the Hodge Conjecture conditionally using CM specialization and variational Hodge theory. At t = 0, the curve C_0: y^2 = x^9 + x is explicitly dominated by the Fermat curve F_16: u^16 + v^16 = 1 via the rational map x = u^2/v^2, y = u/v^9. By Shioda's theorem (1982), the exotic Weil class is algebraic at t = 0. Since Paper 85 proved the exotic trace tau_+ = 0 over Q(t) (the class is a global flat section), the Variational Hodge Conjecture spreads algebraicity from the CM fiber to the generic fiber.

The constructive content (BISH) establishes the Fermat domination, trace vanishing, and non-palindromic obstruction. The classical content (CLASS) is isolated to exactly two axioms: Shioda's Fermat Hodge Conjecture and the Variational Hodge Conjecture. Tenth CRMLint Squeeze.
