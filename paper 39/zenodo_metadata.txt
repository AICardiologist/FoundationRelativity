DESCRIPTION:
Paper 39 (v2) in the Constructive Reverse Mathematics of Mathematical Physics series. While Papers 36-38 showed all known physical undecidabilities are LPO (Sigma-1-zero), this paper proves the ceiling is not Sigma-1-zero. A modified Cubitt encoding, running a Turing machine on all inputs simultaneously via Robinson tiling with perimeter counters, encodes the Sigma-2-zero-complete Finiteness Problem into the spectral gap. The generic spectral gap decision (without promise gap) is Sigma-2-zero/Pi-2-zero-complete, requiring LPO_jump (the Turing jump of LPO). The exact zero-test for any decreasing non-negative sequence (extensive or intensive) is Sigma-2-zero: deciding whether the limit is positive or zero requires the nested quantifier witness, which is LPO_jump. The Physical Stratification Theorem: the arithmetic complexity of a physical observable is determined by its epistemic mode, not thermodynamic scaling. Platonic exact physics (no promise gap) reaches LPO_jump (Sigma-2-zero). Promise-gapped physics and empirical physics (finite precision) cap at LPO (Sigma-1-zero). v2 erratum: v1 incorrectly claimed extensive observables cap at LPO and used Bool instead of Prop in the LPO_jump definition. Both errors corrected. All results formalized in 873 lines of Lean 4 over Mathlib with zero sorry. 14 pages, 21 references, 8 Lean source files.
