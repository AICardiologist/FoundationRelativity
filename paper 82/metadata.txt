Title: Picard-Fuchs Monodromy via Kovacic's Algorithm: A CRMLint Squeeze (Paper 82, Constructive Reverse Mathematics Series)

Author: Paul Chun-Kit Lee

Affiliation: New York University, Brooklyn, New York

Date: 2026-02-26

Description:
Paper 82 of the Constructive Reverse Mathematics (CRM) Series computes the
differential Galois group G_gal = SL_2 for the Legendre Picard-Fuchs equation
t(1-t)y'' + (1-2t)y' - (1/4)y = 0 using Kovacic's algorithm, entirely within
Bishop-style constructive mathematics (BISH).

The classical proof of G_gal = SL_2 proceeds via topological monodromy
representations, fundamental groups, analytic continuation, and Zariski density
arguments -- all requiring classical logic (CLASS). This paper replaces the
entire topological apparatus with rational function analysis over Q(t): normal
form reduction (polynomial identity), pole classification and indicial equations
(rational arithmetic), and Kovacic's three-case algorithm (finite case checking).
All three Kovacic cases fail by rational arithmetic, establishing G_gal = SL_2
without any classical content.

This is the fifth CRMLint Squeeze in the series and the third paper in the
function-field pipeline: Paper 80 (Gauss-Manin connection by Griffiths pole
reduction), Paper 81 (flat sections and algebraic Fixed Part theorem), Paper 82
(differential Galois group by Kovacic). Together, the three papers realize the
Tannakian reconstruction of a geometric motive by constructive means.

The Lean 4 formalization comprises 457 lines with zero sorry. The main squeeze
theorem depends only on kernel axioms (propext, Classical.choice, Quot.sound);
the classical boundary node (topological monodromy density) is declared but
never used by any constructive proof.
