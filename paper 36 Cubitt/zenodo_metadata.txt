DESCRIPTION:
Paper 36 in the Constructive Reverse Mathematics of Mathematical Physics series. The celebrated result of Cubitt, Perez-Garcia, and Wolf (2015) showed that determining whether a many-body quantum Hamiltonian is gapped or gapless is undecidable. This paper calibrates that undecidability as Turing-Weihrauch equivalent to LPO (the Limited Principle of Omniscience). The key insight: macroscopic quantum undecidability costs exactly one thermodynamic limit, the same logical price paid for phase transitions, continuum limits, and all other infinite-volume completions across physics.

The stratification reveals: (i) finite-volume spectral gap is BISH-computable; (ii) the thermodynamic limit costs LPO; (iii) pointwise decidability requires LPO; (iv) gap zero-test costs WLPO (using the negative disjunct: Delta = 0 or Delta != 0, not Delta > 0; the positive formulation would be LPO); (v) uniform decidability costs LPO. The WLPO/LPO distinction is subtle: for non-negative reals, deciding Delta = 0 v Delta > 0 is LPO, while deciding Delta = 0 v Delta != 0 is WLPO; passing from != 0 to > 0 requires Markov's Principle, and WLPO + MP = LPO.

All results formalized in Lean 4 over Mathlib with zero sorry. The formalization comprises 8 Lean source files (667 lines total). Lean 4 toolchain v4.28.0-rc1.
