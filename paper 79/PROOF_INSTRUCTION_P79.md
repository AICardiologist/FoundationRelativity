### The Problem
Standard Conjecture D asserts that numerical equivalence (intersection pairing against all cycles is 0) implies homological equivalence (the cohomology class is 0). This is equivalent to saying the intersection pairing is non-degenerate on the Q-vector space of algebraic cycles.

### The Forest (BISH)

If we compute the Gram matrix of the cup product on the entire exotic Hodge subspace and prove it is definite via Sylvester's Criterion (exact rational arithmetic), Conjecture D is unconditionally true for the unknown algebraic cycles.

### The Primitive Cohomology Insight (Crucial)
If you project the intersection pairing strictly onto the orthogonal complement of the divisor classes (the Exotic Subspace), any class x in this space is orthogonal to L wedge y for all divisors y (where L is the ample polarization divisor). Because integral(x wedge (L wedge y)) = integral((L wedge x) wedge y) = 0 for all y, it follows that L wedge x = 0. This means the entire Exotic Subspace consists of primitive cohomology classes. By the transcendental Hodge-Riemann bilinear relations, the intersection pairing on primitive (2,2) classes is strictly definite (either positive or negative). A strictly definite bilinear form is unconditionally non-degenerate on every subspace. Therefore, if we compute the exact rational matrix and verify its definiteness, we prove Conjecture D without ever needing to know what the algebraic cycles are.
