Title: The Squeeze Is a Microscope: Automated Constructivization from CRMLint to the Hodge Horizon (Paper 90, Constructive Reverse Mathematics Series)

Author: Paul Chun-Kit Lee

Affiliation: New York University, Brooklyn, New York

Date: February 2026

Description: Synthesis monograph covering the generative phase (Papers 76-89) of the Constructive Reverse Mathematics program. Documents the CRMLint Squeeze protocol, a four-step procedure that isolates the constructive core of classical theorems. Across eleven Squeeze applications spanning Hodge theory, the Langlands program, differential Galois theory, and algebraic geometry, the protocol decomposes classical proofs into BISH-verified components (polynomial identities, dimension data, trace computations — all checked by Lean 4's ring and decide tactics) and irreducible CLASS boundary nodes (existence-of-algebraic-cycle assertions). The Hodge campaign (Papers 84-89) applies the Squeeze to exotic Weil classes on genus-4 hyperelliptic Jacobians, revealing a bifurcation: unconditional algebraicity on the palindromic locus via Kani-Rosen splitting, and CLASS-conditional algebraicity on the generic complement via the Variational Hodge Conjecture. Paper 87's calibration shows the uniform Hodge test is equivalent to WLPO, explaining why all known Hodge results require algebraic metadata. Reports aggregate Lean 4 statistics: approximately 5,300 lines across 12 papers, zero sorry, verified on Mathlib toolchain v4.28.0-rc1.

Keywords: constructive reverse mathematics, CRMLint, Hodge conjecture, Weil classes, Variational Hodge Conjecture, Bishop constructive mathematics, Lean 4, formal verification, omniscience hierarchy, Kani-Rosen splitting, Gauss-Manin connection
