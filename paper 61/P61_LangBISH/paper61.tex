\title{Lang's Conjecture as the MP~$\to$~BISH Gate: \\
The Decidability Hierarchy for Mixed Motives}
\author{Paul Lee}
\date{February 2026}

\begin{document}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
Paper~60 established that the decidability of $\Ext^1(\Q(0), M)$ for motives with the Northcott property is stratified by analytic rank: BISH for $r \le 1$, MP for $r \ge 2$.  We prove that an effective Lang Height Lower Bound Conjecture is the precise gate converting the rank~$\ge 2$ regime from MP to BISH, via inversion of Minkowski's Second Theorem.  The implication is strict: BISH-decidability does not imply Lang, because constructive computability imposes no constraint on the geometric decay rate of minimal heights.  We verify the forward direction explicitly for $X_0(389)$ (rank~2).  For motives lacking the Northcott property (K3 surfaces, higher K-theory), we prove the decidability requirement escalates from MP to LPO: verifying lattice completeness over an infinite bounded-height cycle space requires universal quantification equivalent to the Limited Principle of Omniscience.  The full hierarchy is thus:
\[
\text{BISH} \;\subsetneq\; \text{MP} \;\subsetneq\; \text{LPO}
\]
with Lang's conjecture gating MP~$\to$~BISH and the Northcott property gating LPO~$\to$~MP.  Under the Uniform Lang--Silverman Conjecture, the BISH search bound becomes a function exclusively of the $L$-function, establishing the $L$-function as a universal analytic decidability certificate.

All theorems are formally verified in Lean~4 with Mathlib.  Zero \texttt{sorry}s.  The axiom profile is: \texttt{propext}, \texttt{Quot.sound}, and infrastructure \texttt{Classical.choice} from Mathlib's $\Q$ library; plus one open-conjecture axiom (\texttt{UniformLang}) used only for Theorem~E.
\end{abstract}

% =====================================================================
\section{Background and context}
% =====================================================================

Paper~60 proved that the analytic rank $r = \ord_{s = s_0} L(M, s)$ stratifies the logical complexity of computing $\Ext^1(\Q(0), M)$:
\begin{itemize}
\item $r = 0$: $\Ext^1 = 0$, verified by checking $L(M, s_0) \ne 0$ to finite precision.  BISH.
\item $r = 1$: The $L$-derivative determines the regulator $R(M) = \hhat(P)$, bounding the search space via Northcott.  BISH (conditional on Bloch--Kato).
\item $r \ge 2$: The $L$-function gives only the regulator determinant $R(M) = \det(\langle P_i, P_j \rangle)$.  Minkowski's geometry of numbers: a lattice covolume in dimension~$\ge 2$ does not bound individual basis vectors.  MP.
\end{itemize}

The present paper asks: what converts the $r \ge 2$ regime from MP to BISH?

% =====================================================================
\section{Lang's conjecture inverts the Minkowski bound}
% =====================================================================

\begin{definition}[Effective Lang]
\label{def:lang}
An \emph{effective Lang Height Lower Bound} for an abelian variety $A/\Q$ of dimension~$g$ is a computable constant $c = c(A) > 0$ such that
\[
\hhat(P) \ge c \quad \text{for all non-torsion } P \in A(\Q).
\]
\end{definition}

Let $\Lambda \subset \R^r$ be the Mordell--Weil lattice of rank~$r$, equipped with the canonical height pairing.  Let $\lambda_1 \le \lambda_2 \le \cdots \le \lambda_r$ be the successive minima.  By Minkowski's Second Theorem:
\[
\lambda_1 \cdot \lambda_2 \cdots \lambda_r \le \gamma_r^{r/2} \cdot \sqrt{R}
\]
where $\gamma_r$ is the Hermite constant and $R = \det(\langle P_i, P_j \rangle)$ is the regulator.

\begin{theorem}[Lang $\Rightarrow$ BISH]
\label{thm:lang-bish}
If Effective Lang (Definition~\ref{def:lang}) holds with constant~$c$, then for any rank~$r \ge 2$ Mordell--Weil lattice with regulator~$R$:
\[
\hhat_{\max} := \lambda_r \;\le\; \frac{\gamma_r^{r/2} \cdot \sqrt{R}}{c^{\,r-1}}.
\]
Combined with Northcott's theorem, this provides a computable finite search set for all generators.  The unbounded MP search becomes bounded BISH verification.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By Lang, $\lambda_i \ge c$ for $i = 1, \ldots, r-1$.  Substituting into Minkowski:
\[
c^{r-1} \cdot \lambda_r \;\le\; \gamma_r^{r/2} \cdot \sqrt{R}.
\]
Dividing by $c^{r-1} > 0$ yields the bound.  Since $\gamma_r$, $R$, and $c$ are all computable, $\hhat_{\max}$ is computable.  By the Northcott property for abelian varieties, the set $\{P \in A(\Q) : \hhat(P) \le \hhat_{\max}\}$ is finite and explicitly enumerable.  Exhaustive search over this finite set decides the generators in bounded time.
\end{proof}

% =====================================================================
\section{The converse fails}
% =====================================================================

\begin{theorem}[BISH $\not\Rightarrow$ Lang]
\label{thm:converse}
BISH-decidability of the Mordell--Weil generators does not imply Lang's conjecture.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
BISH requires only the existence of a computable bounding function $B(A)$ for each abelian variety~$A$.  Lang's conjecture requires a \emph{geometrically constrained} lower bound $c(A) \ge C \cdot \Delta(A)^{-\alpha}$ for explicit constants $C, \alpha$ depending only on the dimension.

These are different conditions.  Consider the family $c(n) = 1/(n+2)$.  For each~$n$, a BISH bounding function exists (each curve's search is finite).  But $\inf_n c(n) = 0$, violating any uniform Lang bound.  The Lean proof constructs the explicit witness: for any proposed $C > 0$, taking $n$ with $n + 2 > 1/C$ yields $c(n) < C$, contradiction.
\end{proof}

\section{Explicit verification: $X_0(389)$}
% =====================================================================

\begin{example}[$X_0(389)$, rank 2]
\label{ex:389}
The elliptic curve $E = X_0(389)$ has analytic rank~2 with:
\[
R \approx 0.15246, \qquad \gamma_2 = \tfrac{4}{3}, \qquad c(E) \approx 0.0494 \;\text{(Hindry--Silverman)}.
\]

Applying Theorem~\ref{thm:lang-bish} with $\sqrt{R} \approx 0.3904$:
\[
\hhat_{\max} = \frac{\gamma_2 \cdot \sqrt{R}}{c(E)} \approx \frac{(4/3) \cdot 0.3904}{0.0494} \approx 10.54.
\]
The known generators $P_1 = (0, 0)$ and $P_2 = (-1, 1)$ have canonical heights $\hhat(P_1) \approx 0.327$ and $\hhat(P_2) \approx 0.465$, both well within the bound.

The Lean formalization verifies this with rational arithmetic: \texttt{generators\_within\_bound} confirms $\hhat(P_i) \le \hhat_{\max}$ for $i = 1, 2$ via \texttt{norm\_num}.
\end{example}

\section{Northcott failure escalates to LPO}
% =====================================================================

\begin{theorem}[No Northcott $\Rightarrow$ LPO]
\label{thm:lpo}
For motives whose cycle space lacks the Northcott property (e.g., algebraic cycles on K3 surfaces, higher algebraic K-theory classes), decidability of the $\Ext^1$ lattice generators at rank~$\ge 2$ requires the Limited Principle of Omniscience.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Without Northcott, the set of cycles with canonical height $\le B$ is infinite for any~$B > 0$.  Given candidate generators $g_1, \ldots, g_r$, verifying that they generate the \emph{full} lattice (not merely a finite-index sublattice) requires:
\[
\forall\, z \in \{z : \hhat(z) \le B\}, \quad z \in \Z g_1 + \cdots + \Z g_r.
\]
For each individual~$z$, membership in the $\Z$-span is decidable (integer linear algebra).  But the universal quantification ranges over an infinite set.  This infinite conjunction is constructively equivalent to LPO: given any sequence $\alpha : \mathbb{N} \to \{0,1\}$, define cycle $z_n$ to lie in the span iff $\alpha(n) = 0$.  Then:
\[
\forall n,\; z_n \in \Z\text{-span}(g_1, \ldots, g_r) \;\;\Longleftrightarrow\;\; \forall n,\; \alpha(n) = 0.
\]
The Lean proof establishes the full equivalence:
\[
\bigl(\forall\, \text{membership} : \mathbb{N} \to \{0,1\},\; (\forall n,\, m(n)=1) \lor (\exists n,\, m(n)=0)\bigr) \;\Longleftrightarrow\; \text{LPO}.
\]
\end{proof}

This extends the Paper~60 hierarchy to three tiers:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{llll}
\toprule
\textbf{Rank} & \textbf{Northcott?} & \textbf{Logic} & \textbf{Gate to BISH} \\
\midrule
$r = 0$ & --- & BISH & --- \\
$r = 1$ & Yes & BISH & --- \\
$r \ge 2$ & Yes & MP & Lang's conjecture \\
$r \ge 2$ & No & LPO & Northcott + Lang \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\section{Uniform Lang and the analytic certificate}
% =====================================================================

\begin{theorem}[Uniform Lang $\Rightarrow$ analytic BISH]
\label{thm:uniform}
Under the Uniform Lang--Silverman Conjecture (the lower bound $c$ depends only on the dimension~$g$ and the number field~$K$, not on the specific variety), the BISH search bound becomes:
\[
\hhat_{\max} = \frac{\gamma_r^{r/2} \cdot \sqrt{R}}{c(g, K)^{\,r-1}}
\]
where $R$ is computable from $L^{(r)}(M, s_0)$ via the Bloch--Kato conjecture.  The bound depends exclusively on the $L$-function and universal constants.
\end{theorem}

\begin{remark}
Under Uniform Lang, a single Turing machine processes the $L$-function of any abelian variety of dimension~$g$ over~$K$ and halts with the Mordell--Weil generators, without parsing the specific moduli, discriminant, or Faltings height.  The $L$-function is the universal analytic decidability certificate.  This is the strongest form of the DPT thesis: the motive's analytic realization natively certificates its own arithmetic decidability.
\end{remark}

\section{Conclusion}
% =====================================================================

The DPT decidability hierarchy for mixed motives is governed by two independent gates:

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Gate 1: Northcott.}  Controls LPO~$\to$~MP.  Abelian varieties pass (Northcott holds).  K3 surfaces and higher K-theory fail (bounded height contains infinitely many cycles).

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Gate 2: Lang.}  Controls MP~$\to$~BISH.  Provides a computable lower bound on generator heights, converting Minkowski's covolume inequality into an individual vector bound.  The implication is strict: Lang~$\Rightarrow$~BISH but BISH~$\not\Rightarrow$~Lang.

\medskip
\noindent Under Uniform Lang, the $L$-function alone determines the search bound, establishing the $L$-function as a universal decidability certificate for the arithmetic of motives.  The pure motive programme (Papers~50--59) is complete.  The mixed motive programme (Papers~60--61) identifies the precise logical gates separating BISH, MP, and LPO for $\Ext^1$.
