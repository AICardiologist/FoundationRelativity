Title: Algebraic Gauss-Manin via Griffiths Pole Reduction: A CRMLint Squeeze (Paper 80, Constructive Reverse Mathematics Series)

Author: Paul Chun-Kit Lee

Affiliation: New York University

Date: February 2026

Description:
Paper 80 of the Constructive Reverse Mathematics Series upgrades the CRMLint Squeeze protocol from exact arithmetic over Q (Papers 77-78) to symbolic algebra over the function field Q(t). The target is the Legendre family of elliptic curves y^2 = x(x-1)(x-t). The Classical Boundary Node is the Ehresmann-Gauss-Manin theorem, which requires Ehresmann's fibration theorem, multi-valued functions, and transcendental analysis (CLASS). The constructive path computes the Gauss-Manin connection matrix via Griffiths pole reduction in algebraic de Rham cohomology over Q[t]. A Python CAS performs the pole reduction and emits polynomial identities; Lean 4 verifies them via the ring tactic (0 sorry, 0 axioms on the constructive path).

CRM descent: CLASS (Ehresmann-Gauss-Manin) to BISH (polynomial algebra over Q[t], verified by ring). This is an infrastructure upgrade: ring over Q(t) replaces native_decide over Z, enabling all subsequent papers (81-94) to work at the function-field level. Third CRMLint Squeeze application (after Papers 77, 78).

Lean 4 formalization: 523 lines (278 CAS-emitted + 245 architecture), 0 sorry. Axiom inventory: propext, Classical.choice, Quot.sound (Lean kernel only); ehresmann_gauss_manin_existence does not appear in #print axioms gauss_manin_squeeze.

Keywords: constructive reverse mathematics, Gauss-Manin connection, Griffiths pole reduction, algebraic de Rham cohomology, Picard-Fuchs equation, Legendre family, elliptic curves, function field, Lean 4, formal verification, CRMLint Squeeze, BISH
