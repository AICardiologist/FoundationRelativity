\begin{abstract}
We introduce the \emph{CRMLint Squeeze}, a systematic protocol for
reverse-engineering classical proofs into constructive ones by
combining CRM logical cost analysis with AI-driven combinatorial
search.  The method identifies the \emph{Classical Boundary Node}
(CBN)---the exact lemma where a classical proof invokes an
omniscience principle---excises it from the Lean~4 environment,
and tasks a reinforcement-learning-trained prover with closing the
resulting gap using only~$\BISH$-admissible tactics.  The CRM
classification acts as a reward function: $+10$ for a $\BISH$
closure, $-1$ for any branch introducing $\CLASS$ dependencies.
This forces the AI into the bounded algebraic search space where
Monte Carlo Tree Search achieves superhuman performance.

We apply the protocol to Greg Anderson's exotic Weil classes on
CM abelian fourfolds~$E^4$ \cite{Anderson1993}.  The Hodge
Conjecture guarantees an algebraic cycle exists for these classes
($\CLASS$), but provides no construction.  Finding the cycle is a
bounded $\Q$-linear algebra problem in the Chow group---a finite
combinatorial search over intersection products of abelian
subvarieties, CM endomorphism graphs, and twisted diagonal
embeddings.  We define the Lean~4 architecture that separates the
classical axiom from the constructive $\Sigma$-type target,
establish the restricted generator set, and characterise the
search space.

This paper marks the transition of the CRM program from
\emph{diagnostic} (auditing classical proofs, Papers~1--76) to
\emph{generative}: using the logical cost metric as a loss
function that forces the production of explicit algebraic
structures.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}\label{sec:intro}
% ============================================================

\subsection{The helicopter and the forest}

Human mathematicians use classical principles---Zorn's Lemma,
uncountable limits, topological compactness---as \emph{compression
algorithms}.  A human brain cannot hold a 10,000-term polynomial
or a $70 \times 70$ intersection matrix in working memory.
Classical existence theorems allow the mathematician to fly over
the algebraic thicket and declare ``a solution exists,'' leaving
behind an ineffective result.  We call this the \emph{classical
helicopter}.

AI models are the exact opposite.  They are poor at abstract,
infinite-dimensional topological jumps (they hallucinate), but
possess effectively infinite working memory for navigating massive,
dense combinatorial thickets.  If the classical axioms are enabled,
the AI lazily mimics humans and takes the helicopter.  But if
CRMLint systematically bans the helicopter, the AI is
algorithmically cornered into searching the algebraic forest---the
exact domain where Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) achieves
superhuman performance.

The CRMLint Squeeze exploits this asymmetry: \emph{human geometric
intuition sets the boundaries; CRMLint acts as the electric fence;
AI combinatorial search builds the bridge.}

\subsection{The Goldilocks zone}

The method works only in the \emph{Goldilocks zone}: conjectures
where the classical helicopter has already landed (an ineffective
$\CLASS$ proof of existence), or where the cliff boundary is
strictly narrowed by adjacent theorems.  Pointing an AI at the
empty void of a totally open problem yields infinite search width
and certain failure.  The CRM atlas (Papers~1--76)
identifies the narrow-cliff targets.

\subsection{The CRM hierarchy}

The constructive hierarchy of omniscience principles:
\[
  \BISH \;\subset\; \BISH{+}\MP \;\subset\; \BISH{+}\LLPO
  \;\subset\; \BISH{+}\WLPO \;\subset\; \BISH{+}\LPO
  \;\subset\; \CLASS.
\]
For definitions and the programme's calibration data, see
Paper~50~\cite{Paper50}.

\subsection{CRMLint}

CRMLint (Paper~76~\cite{Paper76}) is an automated logical cost
analyzer for Lean~4 formalizations.  It operates in four layers:

\begin{enumerate}[nosep]
\item \emph{Dependency tracer.} BFS traversal of the Lean~4
  \texttt{Environment}, tracing transitive paths to every
  \texttt{Classical.choice}, \texttt{Classical.em},
  \texttt{propext}, \texttt{Quot.sound} callsite.
\item \emph{Pattern classifier.} 12-rule priority classifier
  with root-context fallback, distinguishing infrastructure
  artifacts from essential classical content.
\item \emph{Concentration analysis.} Identifies the minimal
  essential classical core of each theorem.
\item \emph{AI audit layer.} Predicts conservation gap
  eliminability using the 75-paper programme as training data.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Classical Boundary Nodes}

\begin{definition}[Classical Boundary Node]\label{def:cbn}
Let $T$ be a theorem with CRM classification $\CLASS$ (or
$\WLPO$, $\LPO$).  A \emph{Classical Boundary Node}
$\CBN(T)$ is a lemma $L$ in the dependency DAG of~$T$ such that:
\begin{enumerate}[nosep]
\item $L$ itself invokes a classical principle (directly or
  through one step), and
\item every dependency of $L$ that is not infrastructure is
  $\BISH$-classified.
\end{enumerate}
The CBN is the \emph{shallowest point} at which classical content
enters the proof.  Excising the CBN and all paths through it
produces a $\BISH$ environment with an open goal.
\end{definition}

\subsection{The Anderson obstruction}

Anderson~\cite{Anderson1993} (see also Weil~\cite{Weil1977})
proved that products of CM elliptic curves of dimension $\geq 4$
support \emph{exotic Weil classes}: Hodge classes not generated by
divisors.  Paper~50~\cite{Paper50} showed that:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
\item CM elliptic curves ($\dim = 1$) are unconditionally
  $\BISH$-decidable (Theorem~E).
\item The Anderson obstruction begins at $\dim = 4$.
\item The Hodge Conjecture for these classes is open.
\end{itemize}
The DPT framework detects this boundary: CM elliptic curves are
in; CM abelian fourfolds are out.

\section{The CRMLint Squeeze Protocol}\label{sec:protocol}
% ============================================================

\subsection{Step~1: The Scaffold}

Take a known classical theorem (or a partial $\CLASS$ attempt)
and write its outline in Lean~4.  The proof need not be complete;
what matters is that the \emph{logical structure} is formalised:
which lemmas invoke which principles, and where the classical
content enters.

\subsection{Step~2: The X-Ray (CRMLint)}

Run CRMLint on the scaffold.  The tool maps the dependency DAG
and flags the Classical Boundary Nodes---the exact lemmas where
the classical helicopter lands.

\begin{protocol}[X-Ray]\label{prot:xray}
\begin{lstlisting}
-- Run CRMLint on the target theorem
#crm_audit hodge_conjecture_E4

-- Expected output:
-- hodge_conjecture_E4 : CLASS
--   CBN: hodge_conjecture_existence (1 step to Classical.choice)
--   All other dependencies: BISH
\end{lstlisting}
\end{protocol}

\subsection{Step~3: The Excise}

Isolate the local Lean state at the CBN.  Delete the classical
axiom and apply \texttt{[-Classical]} to the environment.  The
result is a $\BISH$ context with an open goal of the form
\[
  \vdash \Sigma\,(Z : \CH^2(A)),\; \cl(Z) = w_{\mathrm{exotic}}.
\]

\emph{Key design choice:} The target is a $\Sigma$-type (data),
not a $\exists$ (proposition).  This forces the AI to
\emph{construct} the cycle, not merely prove its existence.

\subsection{Step~4: The Squeeze}

Hand the isolated state to an RL-trained prover.  The CRMLint
classification acts as the reward function:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lc}
\toprule
\textbf{Event} & \textbf{Reward} \\
\midrule
Goal closed with $\BISH$ classification & $+10.0$ \\
Proof step introduces $\CLASS$ dependency & $\times(-1.0)$ \\
$\WLPO$ dependency (marginal cost) & $\times(+0.5)$ \\
Timeout / failure & $0.0$ \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

Because the AI knows the theorem is true but is barred from the
classical shortcut, its MCTS is structurally forced to invent a
new algebraic witness: a bounding polynomial, a finite matrix
identity, or an explicit invariant.  That witness is novel,
publishable mathematics.

\section{The Target: Anderson's Exotic Weil Classes on $E^4$}%
\label{sec:target}
% ============================================================

\subsection{Setup}

Let $E/\Q$ be a CM elliptic curve with complex multiplication by
an imaginary quadratic order $\calO_K \subset K = \Q(\sqrt{-d})$.
Set $A = E^4 = E \times E \times E \times E$, a CM abelian
fourfold.

The singular cohomology $H^*(A, \Q)$ is generated by pullbacks
from $H^1(E, \Q) \cong \Q^2$.  Write $e_1, e_2$ for a basis of
$H^1(E, \Q)$.  The cohomology ring is
\[
  H^*(A, \Q) \;\cong\; \bigwedge\nolimits^* (e_{1,1}, e_{2,1},
  \ldots, e_{1,4}, e_{2,4}),
\]
where $e_{j,i}$ denotes the pullback of $e_j$ from the $i$-th
factor.

\subsection{The exotic Weil class}

The CM endomorphism $\alpha \in \calO_K$ acts on $H^1(E, \Q)$ by
a $2 \times 2$ matrix $M_\alpha$.  On $H^4(A, \Q)$, the
4-fold wedge products of the eigenspaces of $M_\alpha$ produce
Hodge classes that are \emph{not} in the $\Q$-span of products of
divisor classes.  Anderson~\cite{Anderson1993} showed:

\begin{theorem}[Anderson 1993]\label{thm:anderson}
There exist Hodge classes in $H^{2,2}(E^4) \cap H^4(E^4, \Q)$
that do not lie in the subring generated by divisor classes.
These are the \emph{exotic Weil classes}.
\end{theorem}

\subsection{The classical cliff}

The Hodge Conjecture asserts:
\[
  \forall\, w \in H^{p,p}(X) \cap H^{2p}(X, \Q),\;\;
  \exists\, Z \in \CH^p(X)_\Q \text{ such that }
  \cl(Z) = w.
\]
For $w = w_{\mathrm{exotic}}$ on $E^4$, this is the
CBN---a $\CLASS$ existential with no constructive witness.

\subsection{The restricted generator set}

Finding the cycle requires searching within the subring of
$\CH^2(E^4)_\Q$ generated by:

\begin{enumerate}[nosep]
\item \textbf{Projections.} $p_i : E^4 \to E_i$ and their
  graphs $\Gamma_{p_i} \subset E^4$.
\item \textbf{CM endomorphism graphs.}
  $\Gamma_\alpha \subset E_i \times E_j$ for
  $\alpha \in \calO_K$, embedded in $E^4$ via the inclusion
  $E_i \times E_j \hookrightarrow E^4$.
\item \textbf{Twisted diagonal embeddings.}
  $\Delta_{\alpha,\beta} \subset E_i \times E_j \times E_k$
  defined by $(x, \alpha(x), \beta(x))$, embedded in $E^4$.
\end{enumerate}

The search space is the $\Q$-linear span of intersection
products of these generators in $\CH^2(E^4)_\Q$.  This is a
\emph{finite-dimensional} $\Q$-vector space---the exact domain
where MCTS is maximally efficient.

\subsection{Asymmetric offloading}

The CRMLint Squeeze exploits a three-way division of labor:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
\item \emph{Human geometric intuition} identifies the target,
  defines the exotic Weil class, and restricts the generator set.
\item \emph{CRMLint} acts as the electric fence, ensuring the AI
  constructs an explicit algebraic witness rather than
  hallucinating an analytic shortcut.
\item \emph{AI working memory} performs the massive $\Q$-linear
  matrix algebra required to align the cycle class with the exotic
  cohomology class.
\end{itemize}

\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}
% ============================================================

The CRMLint Squeeze transitions the CRM program from auditing
the history of mathematics to engineering its future.  The
logical cost metric becomes a loss function that forces the
production of explicit algebraic structures.  We do not ask the
AI to invent a new helicopter; we use the CRM classification to
force the AI to stack algebraic stones until a bridge is formed.

The first target---Anderson's exotic Weil classes on CM abelian
fourfolds---is chosen because the search space is strictly
bounded, the classical cliff is precisely identified, and the
generator set is geometrically natural.  Success would provide the
first explicitly constructed algebraic cycle for an exotic Weil
class in the history of mathematics: a 33-year-old problem
reduced to a single, verifiable, $\BISH$-decidable formula.
