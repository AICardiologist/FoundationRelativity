Abstract:
We introduce the CRMLint Squeeze, a systematic protocol for reverse-engineering classical proofs into constructive ones by combining CRM logical cost analysis with computational algebra. The method identifies the Classical Boundary Node (CBN) -- the exact lemma where a classical proof invokes an omniscience principle -- excises it, and replaces it with an explicit algebraic witness verified by compiled rational arithmetic (native_decide).

We execute the protocol on CM abelian fourfolds E^4 with End(E) = Z[i]. The initial target (exotic Weil classes) is computationally refuted: we prove that dim(Hodge^{2,2}) = dim(div. products) = 36, so E^4 supports no exotic Hodge classes (Theorem A). Pivoting to the correct target, we prove the Complete Constructive Hodge Theorem for E^4: every Hodge (2,2) class is an explicit Q-linear combination of cup products of divisor classes, with all 36 basis decompositions verified by native_decide in Lean 4 (Theorem B). The Lean bundle builds with 0 sorry, 0 axiom in the constructive path, and 0 noncomputable.

The paper introduces asymmetric offloading (Python CAS computes, Lean kernel verifies) and documents the token overflow problem and its resolution through sparse match encoding. This marks the transition of the CRM program from diagnostic (Papers 1-76) to generative, continuing the arc from Paper 75 (conservation gap detection) through Paper 76 (CRMLint automated analysis) to Paper 77 (automated constructivisation).
