Title: The Structural Vanishing Theorem: Why Exotic Weil Classes Are Universally Flat (Paper 93 of the Constructive Reverse Mathematics Series)

Author: Paul Chun-Kit Lee

Affiliation: New York University, Brooklyn, New York

Date: 2026-02-28

Description:
Papers 84-92 of this series verified computationally that the Gauss-Manin trace on the determinant bundle of the sigma-eigenspace vanishes identically for odd hyperelliptic Weil families in genera 2 through 8. This paper provides two independent structural proofs explaining this vanishing. Proof 1 uses the Aomoto-Varchenko period determinant formula: the substitution u = x^2 reduces the eigenspace to H^1 of a fractional local system, whose root-root Varchenko exponents cancel to zero (discriminant drops out) while the root-origin exponent is frozen at -1/4 by Vieta's formula applied to the palindromic-adjacent polynomial P(u) = u^g + ... + 1. Proof 2 uses Deligne regularity and Picard-Lefschetz theory: the trace form can only have logarithmic poles along the discriminant and boundary divisors; unipotent monodromy kills the discriminant term, and the unit coefficient constraint kills the boundary terms. The algebraic backbone (exponent arithmetic, Vieta's formula, intersection numbers, boundary freezing) is verified in Lean 4 with Mathlib. The topological inputs are axiom stubs classified as CLASS. CRM score: 13 BISH + 4 CLASS = 17 components.

Keywords: constructive reverse mathematics, Gauss-Manin connection, Weil classes, hyperelliptic curves, Varchenko formula, Deligne regularity, Picard-Lefschetz, Lean 4, formal verification
