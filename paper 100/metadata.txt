Title: The Kuga-Satake Bifurcation: K3 Hodge Classes via Shioda-Inose Descent (Paper 100 of the Constructive Reverse Mathematics Series)

Author: Paul Chun-Kit Lee

Affiliation: New York University, Brooklyn, New York

Date: March 2026

Description:
We analyse the constructive reverse mathematics of the Hodge conjecture for K3 surfaces via the Kuga-Satake construction. The CRM cost bifurcates at Picard number rho = 20: for singular K3 surfaces, the Shioda-Inose structure provides CM elliptic curves and the CM metadata makes all Hodge-theoretic conclusions decidable (BISH); for generic rho < 20, the Mumford-Tate group is maximal and Betti realization is required (CLASS). The CRMLint decomposition yields 11 BISH + 5 CLASS = 16 components (69% BISH), with the bifurcation controlled by a single discrete invariant (the Picard number), exactly paralleling Paper 86's Kani-Rosen bifurcation for hyperelliptic Jacobians.

This is the sixteenth CRMLint application, the first CRM analysis of K3 Hodge theory, and the final paper (Paper 100) of the Constructive Reverse Mathematics Series. The Conservation Conjecture -- that every CLASS theorem with a BISH-statable conclusion admits a lower-level proof -- is stated as the programme's principal open problem, consistent with all evidence from Papers 84-100.

A companion 0-sorry Lean 4 bundle (700 lines) mechanically verifies the CRM framework, including K3 lattice arithmetic, Kuga-Satake dimension formulas, CRM level assignments, and the bifurcation theorem.

Keywords: constructive reverse mathematics, K3 surfaces, Kuga-Satake construction, Shioda-Inose structure, Hodge conjecture, CRMLint, Lean 4, formal verification, Bishop constructive mathematics, Picard number, complex multiplication, Mumford-Tate group, Conservation Conjecture
