Title: The Exotic Trace Probe: Transcendence of Weil Classes via Gauss-Manin Analysis (Paper 84, Constructive Reverse Mathematics Series)

Author: Paul Chun-Kit Lee

Affiliation: New York University, Brooklyn, New York

Date: February 2026

Description: Paper 84 of the Constructive Reverse Mathematics (CRM) Series. We compute the Gauss-Manin connection on the exotic Weil subspace of a genus-4 hyperelliptic family C_t: y^2 = x^9 - tx^5 + x with Q(i)-action. The Q(i)-eigenspace decomposition collapses the 70-dimensional exterior power computation to a scalar ODE via the trace trick. The CAS computation yields tau_+(t) = (-250t^5 + 1890t^3 - 3564t) / (81(t^2 - 4)), with an irregular singularity at infinity (polynomial part of degree 3). The differential Galois group is Gm, certifying that the exotic Weil classes are transcendental over Q(t). No finite base change can produce exotic algebraic cycles generically. CRM Squeeze: CLASS (Hodge Conjecture, comparison theorem, Baire category) descends to BISH (explicit Griffiths-Bezout reduction, trace computation, partial fraction analysis, Galois classification). Seventh CRMLint Squeeze. Lean 4 formalization: 389 lines, 0 sorry. The main squeeze theorem depends on no axioms whatsoever.

Keywords: constructive reverse mathematics, Gauss-Manin connection, exotic Weil classes, Hodge conjecture, differential Galois theory, hyperelliptic curves, abelian fourfolds, Lean 4, formal verification
