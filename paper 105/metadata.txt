Title: The Dynamical Penumbra: CRM of Cardiac Action Potential Dynamics and Defibrillation Certification (Paper 105 of Constructive Reverse Mathematics Series)

Author: Paul Chun-Kit Lee

Affiliation: NYU Grossman School of Medicine, Brooklyn, NY, USA

Date: 2026-03-06

Description:
Paper 105 of the Constructive Reverse Mathematics (CRM) Series.
Clinical Sub-Series Paper C (following Paper 103, Asymptotic Penumbra,
and Paper 104, Algorithmic Penumbra).

We perform a CRM decomposition of cardiac electrophysiology, taking the
FitzHugh-Nagumo model as the paradigmatic example. The paper establishes
an 8-component decomposition:

(1) ODE existence via constructive Picard-Lindelof with explicit Lipschitz
constant L = 202/25 on the trapping box [-3,3] x [-5,5]: BISH.
(2) Trapping region verification by polynomial sign checks: BISH.
(3) Bounded safety verification via SOS barrier certificates: BISH.
(4) Topological charge conservation for spiral waves: BISH.
(5) Eigenvalue sign at fixed rational parameters: BISH.
(6) Hopf bifurcation detection at generic parameters: BISH+WLPO.
(7) Sustained chaotic fibrillation (Cantor Intersection Theorem): BISH+WKL.
(8) Defibrillation threshold optimality (minimax over attractor): BISH+LPO.

Overall: 5 BISH + 1 WLPO + 1 WKL + 1 LPO = 8 components (62.5% BISH).
The logical cost tracks mathematical idealisation (finite to infinite
dimensions), not biological complexity.

Formally verified in Lean 4 (1,246 lines, 9 files, 0 sorry).
