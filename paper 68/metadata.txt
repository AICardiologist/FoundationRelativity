Abstract:
We perform a stage-by-stage constructive reverse mathematics audit of Wiles's proof that the p-adic Galois representation rho_{E,p} attached to every semistable elliptic curve E/Q is modular (Galois modularity: rho_{E,p} isomorphic to rho_{f,p} for some eigenform f), and hence of Fermat's Last Theorem. Geometric modularity (that E is isogenous to A_f) additionally requires Faltings's Isogeny Theorem (CLASS); FLT does not need it, since Ribet's level-lowering operates on the residual representation.

The proof decomposes into five stages: residual modularity (Langlands-Tunnell), deformation ring construction, Hecke algebra construction, the numerical criterion, and Taylor-Wiles patching. Our principal finding is an asymmetry: Stages 2-4 are fully constructive (BISH), while Stage 1 requires WLPO and Stage 5 (Taylor-Wiles patching) requires WKL (inverse limit of finite local rings).

We then show that Stage 1 can be bypassed entirely. The 21st-century proof route (Kisin 2009, Khare-Wintenberger 2009), replacing Wiles's residual prime p=3 with p=2, reduces the base case to dihedral modularity (GL_2(F_2) = S_3), where Hecke theta series provide a BISH construction. The overall classification is CRM(FLT) = WKL. The WLPO was an artefact of Wiles's choice of residual prime; the WKL in patching is irreducible (Paper 98, Calibration Theorem II).

Two post-Wiles developments partially reduce the Stage 5 classification: Brochard's proof of de Smit's conjecture (2017), reducing the freeness verification to finite level, and unconditional effective Chebotarev bounds (Lagarias-Montgomery-Odlyzko 1979, Ahn-Kwon 2019), making the TW prime search a bounded computation. The residual WKL is the inverse limit constructing R_infinity from compatible finite approximations. The Lean 4 verification (493 lines, zero sorry) formalizes the logical assembly.
