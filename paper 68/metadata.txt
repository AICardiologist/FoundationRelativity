Abstract:
We perform a stage-by-stage constructive reverse mathematics audit of Wiles's proof that the p-adic Galois representation rho_{E,p} attached to every semistable elliptic curve E/Q is modular (Galois modularity: rho_{E,p} isomorphic to rho_{f,p} for some eigenform f), and hence of Fermat's Last Theorem. Geometric modularity (that E is isogenous to A_f) additionally requires Faltings's Isogeny Theorem (CLASS); FLT does not need it, since Ribet's level-lowering operates on the residual representation.

The proof decomposes into five stages: residual modularity (Langlands-Tunnell), deformation ring construction, Hecke algebra construction, the numerical criterion, and Taylor-Wiles patching. Our principal finding is an asymmetry: Stages 2-5 are fully constructive (BISH), while Stage 1 requires WLPO. The non-constructive content is concentrated entirely at the entry point -- the analytic theory of weight 1 automorphic forms -- and the Taylor-Wiles engine contributes zero logical cost.

We then show that Stage 1 can be bypassed entirely. The 21st-century proof route (Kisin 2009, Khare-Wintenberger 2009), replacing Wiles's residual prime p=3 with p=2, reduces the base case to dihedral modularity (GL_2(F_2) = S_3), where Hecke theta series (requiring Poisson summation, constructively valid per Bishop 1967) provide a BISH construction. The overall classification of FLT is therefore BISH. The WLPO was an artefact of Wiles's choice of residual prime, not a property of the theorem.

Two post-Wiles developments drive the Stage 5 classification: Brochard's proof of de Smit's conjecture (2017), eliminating the infinite inverse limit, and unconditional effective Chebotarev bounds (Lagarias-Montgomery-Odlyzko 1979, Ahn-Kwon 2019), making the Taylor-Wiles prime search a bounded computation. The Lean 4 verification (493 lines, zero sorry) formalizes the logical assembly.
